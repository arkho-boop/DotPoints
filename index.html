HAPPY SUNDAY -- This week saw fissures emerge in the Liberal-National Coalition over a proposed target of net zero emissions by 2050. The Liberals (particularly the PM) want Australia to adopt the target, but the Nats have a few influential holdouts who see the target as a threat to the bush. Watch this space -- most people think that we will adopt a net-zero by 2050 target, but we're yet to find out the size and scope of the carve-out for the bush the Nats will negotiate.


Elsewhere: The Government voted against an investigation that would have determined whether former cabinet minister Christian Porter had to disclose the source of the secret trust that paid for his legal fight with the ABC for its allegations against him of historical rape. The trust is reportedly valued between $600K and $1M.


Australia is set to deliver booster shots of the Covid-19 vaccines from as early as next week. LOOKING AT: Will ATAGI endorse mixing vaccines? A few countries have started doing this, with the US FDA in the last week giving the green light to mix Pfizer, J&J, and Moderna.

Other interesting stories...

LOCKDOWN ENDS, MORE BEER: Melbourne, Australia. Lockdown partially ended in Melbourne on Saturday midnight, allowing its residents to enjoy the half-open life Sydneysiders and Canberrans have been enjoying for an entire week. Melbourne has been commonly touted in the past month as the most locked-down city in the world. Compulsory beer.

COMPROMISE IS KEY: Washington D.C., U.S.. Following months of impasse in Congress, the Democrats look to have accepted a haircut on Biden's key infrastructure plan, with the price tag set to go down from $3.5T to ~$2T. The demands for the lower price tag came mostly from moderate Democratic senators Joe Manchin and Kyrsten Sinema. The massive plan includes funding for childcare, infrastructure, combatting climate change, and much more. Since the Democrats only have a razer-thin majority in the Senate, they plan on passing this with an arcane Senate procedure, budget reconciliation.

RUSSIAN WINTER: Moscow, Russia. President Putin announced a one-week public holiday from the 30th of October as Russia experiences its biggest spike of Covid-19 yet, recording around 1000 deaths per day. Around 1/3 Russians are fully vaccinated, owing partly to low levels of trust in authorities.

The Economist -- Internet shutdowns have become a weapon of repressive regimes

This is a relatively unknown phenomenon in Western countries.
